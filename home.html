Adoption

So why adopt? Simply put, having a pet is awesome! But if that isn't enough to convince you to adopt one, here are some other wonderful reasons to get a pet from a shelter:

You could literally be saving a life

There are almost three million adoptable animals euthanized in the United States each year because there aren't enough people to adopt them all, and that number only includes dogs and cats. By adopting, you are giving a lost, abandoned, and helpless pet a second chance at a happy life.

You'll save money

Not only are shelter pets often spayed/neutered, but they're usually already up to date on their vaccinations and shots as well. Plus, these pets are often already housetrained and can even do a few tricks!

Pets boost your overall well-being

Pet owners have been linked by the CDC to decreased blood pressure, cholesterol and triglyceride levels, and overall lower stress levels. Pets also give their owner a sense of purpose and are great for combatting loneliness and depression with their unconditional love and companionship.

You're supporting an animal shelter instead of a mass breeding mill

If you don't buy your pet directly from a reputable breeder, you may be purchasing from a mill. Mills are mass breeding facilities where animals are kept in deplorable conditions and forced to reproduce until they are no longer able. When they stop producing, the animals are either killed or abandoned.

In contrast, animal shelters provide a valuable community service. They are non-profit organizations that promote the welfare of all animals and are often staffed by volunteers. Shelters help control the pet population by spaying/neutering their animals as well as by taking the time to place animals in situations where they can be successful pets. Any fees collected by a shelter goes towards the support of this mission.

You'll help create more cage space for other needy animals

Million of pets are homeless because of a situation that was out of their control (i.e. a new baby, move, divorce, or death) and not because of anything they did wrong. In some cases, their owners just weren't interested in training them. By adopting instead of buying your new pet, you will be allowing another homeless pet (especially those that are elderly or special needs) to be taken in off the streets by a shelter.

You'll gain a faithful companion and best friend

Pets are remarkably intelligent. Most shelter pets seem to remember what their lives were like before they were adopted and are endlessly grateful and loving to their new families for taking a chance on them. They tend to be endlessly affectionate and already possess socialization and cooperative skills since they have most likely already lived with a family.

Pet Care

All animals are different, but here are some basic tips for owning any kind of pet:

  1. Get your pet spayed/neutered

    Most animal shelters will have already taken care of this, but make sure your animal will not add to the rampant pet overpopulation problem.

  2. Get your pet examined regularly

    Pets can get sick too. Make sure to get your pet looked at annually by a vet to make sure that they are in good health. Regular vet visits will also help to ensure that your pet is up to date with their vaccines.

  3. Keep your pet active

    In addition to checking their overall health, a vet can also tell you if your pet is maintaining a healthy weight. Many pets are overfed, which can lead to diabetes, arthritis, and even cancer. Make sure your pet has plenty of opportunity to exercise, and double check that you are feeding it the right amount of food.

  4. Keep your pet mentally stimulated

    Pets need to exercise their minds as well as their bodies. Bored pets often become destructive pets. Make sure that doesn't happen by keeping your pet occupied with toys and play time with their humans.

  5. Microchip your pet

    Many pets end up in shelters simply because they got lost one day and couldn't find their way back home. Make sure this doesn't happen to yours by having it microchipped.
